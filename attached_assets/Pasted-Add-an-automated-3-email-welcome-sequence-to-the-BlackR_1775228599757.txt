Add an automated 3-email welcome sequence to the BlackRidge Platforms portal that triggers when a project is marked Active. Also add a manual trigger so I can fire the sequence on any existing project at any time.

TECH STACK

React + Tailwind (existing portal stack)
Supabase for data storage and scheduled jobs
Resend for email delivery
Existing portal auth context and project data structure


SUPABASE TABLE — create if it doesn't exist
welcome_sequences

id (uuid, primary key)
project_id (uuid, foreign key → projects.id)
client_name (text)
client_email (text)
company_name (text)
status (text) — "pending" | "running" | "completed" | "cancelled"
email_1_sent_at (timestamp)
email_2_sent_at (timestamp)
email_3_sent_at (timestamp)
triggered_by (text) — "auto" | "manual"
created_at (timestamp)


TRIGGER 1 — AUTOMATIC
When a project's status is changed to "Active" anywhere in the portal:

Check if a welcome sequence already exists for this project — if one exists and status is "running" or "completed", do nothing
If no sequence exists, automatically create a row in welcome_sequences and start the sequence
Show a small toast notification: "Welcome sequence started for [Client Name]"


TRIGGER 2 — MANUAL
Inside the project detail view, add a "Sequence" tab alongside existing tabs.
Sequence Tab — no sequence started yet:

Empty state: "Start the welcome sequence to begin onboarding this client."
Button: "Start Welcome Sequence" (gold)
Clicking opens a small confirmation modal:

Pre-filled: Client Name, Client Email, Company Name from the project record
Editable in case anything needs to be corrected
Confirm button: "Start Sequence"
On confirm: create row in welcome_sequences, kick off Email 1 immediately, show toast: "Sequence started — Email 1 sent to [email]"



Sequence Tab — sequence running:

Show a timeline view of all three emails with status indicators:

Green checkmark = sent
Gold pulsing dot = scheduled / pending
Gray = not yet scheduled


For each email show: subject line, sent timestamp or scheduled time, status
Button: "Cancel Sequence" — stops any unsent emails, sets status to "cancelled", asks for confirmation before executing
Button: "Resend Email [1/2/3]" on any already-sent email in case of delivery issues

Sequence Tab — sequence completed:

All three checkmarks green
Completed timestamp
Note: "Kickoff form sent as part of Email 3. View responses in the Kickoff tab."
Button: "Restart Sequence" — only if needed, requires confirmation


EMAIL SEQUENCE AND TIMING
All emails sent via Resend. Use Supabase Edge Functions or a scheduled background job to handle the delays. The sequence runs fully automatically once triggered — no manual steps required after the initial trigger.
Email 1 — Send immediately on trigger

Subject: "You made a good call."

Hey [First Name],

No surprises. No guessing. Just clean execution.
Talk soon.

Founder, BlackRidge Platforms


Email 2 — Send 2 hours after Email 1

Subject: "Before we build — a few things worth knowing."
Body:


Hey [First Name],
Before the kickoff form hits your inbox, I want to give you a heads up so you're not scrambling when it arrives.
Start pulling these together now:
Your logo files. Find the highest quality version — ideally SVG or PNG with a transparent background. If you don't have them, no problem, we'll talk about it.
Photos. Professional photos of your team, your space, your work. If you have them, start a folder. If you don't, we'll figure out a plan.
Websites you love. Pull up two or three sites that catch your eye and bookmark them. Doesn't have to be your industry. Just things that feel right.
Logins. Think about what platforms and accounts are tied to your business online. Domain registrar, social media, existing website, Google Business. You don't need to share passwords yet — just know where they live.
One thing I want you to know:
This build is a collaboration. The answers you give me on that form are the foundation everything gets built on. Take your time with it. Be specific. The clients who fill it out thoroughly always end up with something they're proud of.
The form is coming in a couple hours.
Chris
Founder, BlackRidge Platforms


Email 3 — Send 2 hours after Email 2

Subject: "Let's get started — your kickoff form is ready."
Body:


Hey [First Name],
This is it. The form that kicks everything off.
It covers your brand, your pages, your content, your features, your timeline, and everything in between. Takes most people about 10 to 15 minutes if they've got their thoughts together.
A couple things before you dive in:
Be specific. "I want it to look clean and professional" tells me nothing. "I want it to feel like a luxury brand but approachable, like a high-end gym rather than a law firm" tells me everything. The more specific you are the less we go back and forth.
Upload what you have. Even if it's rough. Logos, photos, old brand files, screenshots of things you like. Throw it all in. I'd rather have too much than too little.
If you get stuck on something, skip it and come back. Nothing is permanent. But do your best to fill it out completely before you submit.
[FILL OUT YOUR KICKOFF FORM →] — link to /kickoff/[token] — auto-generate the kickoff token and insert the row in kickoff_submissions at this point if it doesn't already exist
Once I have this back I'll be in touch within one business day with next steps.
Let's build something great.
Chris
Founder, BlackRidge Platforms

IMPORTANT LOGIC NOTES

If the sequence is triggered manually and Email 3 fires, it must auto-generate the kickoff token and create the kickoff_submissions row at that moment if one doesn't already exist — the kickoff link in Email 3 must be a real working URL
If the kickoff form was already sent separately before the sequence ran, use the existing token — don't generate a duplicate
Only one sequence per project — if one is already running or completed, the manual trigger button should reflect that state clearly and offer a restart option instead
If a project already has a status of "Active" when this feature is first deployed, do NOT auto-trigger — only trigger on status change going forward. Manual trigger handles existing projects
All email send attempts should be logged — success or failure — so I can see delivery status in the Sequence tab
