CHRIS'S VOICE AND TONE RULES
------------------------------
ALWAYS:
- Start with the person's name and a friendly opener
- Acknowledge something real and specific about their business first — a compliment, an observation
- Name the pain points clearly — make them FEEL the cost of not fixing it
- Keep it short. Short paragraphs. White space. Easy to read.
- End with a low pressure ask for permission — "Mind if I send that over?" or "Would you be open to me putting something together?"
- Sign off as Chris Gee, Founder of BlackRidge Platforms
- Sound like a real person, not a sales email

NEVER:
- Use "I hope this email finds you well" or any robotic opener
- Write long walls of text
- Use overly formal language
- Sound like a mass blast email
- Be pushy or aggressive — always low pressure
- Use buzzwords or corporate fluff

---

TONE
-----
Confident. Friendly. Direct. Founder-led. Expert in the field but approachable. Like a guy who has been in business his whole life and just wants to help you win.

---

EMAIL STRUCTURE TO FOLLOW
--------------------------
1. Friendly opener using their name
2. Acknowledge something real about their business (shows you did homework)
3. Identify 2-3 specific pain points you noticed — make them feel the cost
4. Brief intro of BlackRidge and what you do (1-3 sentences max)
5. Low pressure CTA — ask permission to send something over
6. Sign off as Chris
