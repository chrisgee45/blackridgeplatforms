PART 2 — TAX CENTER REBUILD
Rebuild the Tax Center into 5 clearly separated sections:
Section 1 — Business Profile (set once on setup)
Fields: Business name, Filing type (Sole Prop / LLC / S-Corp), EIN or SSN, State, Federal tax rate (%), State tax rate (%), Self-employment tax rate (default 15.3%), QBI deduction toggle (20%), Fiscal year start month
Section 2 — Income Summary (auto-calculated, locked — no manual editing)
	∙	Gross Revenue: pulls ONLY from payments table where status = “succeeded”
	∙	Refunds Issued: pulls from any refund records
	∙	Net Gross Income: Gross Revenue minus Refunds
	∙	This section must NEVER pull from CRM deal values or closed-won deal status. Only actual collected payments count. Display a lock icon and tooltip: “This figure is calculated automatically from collected payments.”
Section 3 — Deductions (auto-mapped from expense categories)
Display a clean table: Expense Category | Schedule C Line | Amount | Notes
	∙	Meals & Entertainment: show full amount AND 50% deductible amount side by side
	∙	Vehicle/Mileage: show miles entered × rate = deductible amount
	∙	Health Insurance: show amount with a gold “Self-Employed Deduction” badge
	∙	Client Gifts: show warning badge if any entry exceeds $25
	∙	Total Deductions auto-calculates at the bottom
Section 4 — Quarterly Tax Estimates
This is the most important section for clients. Show a quarterly breakdown card for each quarter:
Q1 — Jan 1 to Mar 31 → Due April 15
Q2 — Apr 1 to May 31 → Due June 15
Q3 — Jun 1 to Aug 31 → Due September 15
Q4 — Sep 1 to Dec 31 → Due January 15 (next year)
Each card shows:
	∙	Income earned that quarter (from payments)
	∙	Estimated tax owed that quarter
	∙	Payment status: Unpaid / Paid (togglable by the user)
	∙	Days until due date (show in red if within 30 days)
	∙	A “Mark as Paid” button that logs the date paid
At the top of this section show a summary banner: “Your next estimated tax payment of $X is due in Y days.”
Section 5 — CPA Export
Three export buttons:
	∙	“Download Schedule C Preview (PDF)” — formatted exactly like IRS Schedule C with all lines populated
	∙	“Download Full P&L (PDF)” — income statement with all revenue and expenses
	∙	“Download Expense Summary (CSV)” — all expenses by category with dates, amounts, notes
