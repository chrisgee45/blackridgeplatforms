The real issue is somewhere in your data pipeline. A few things to check:
Most likely cause: Your balance sheet is calculating from one data source (like manually entered transactions or a specific date range) while RIDGE is pulling from the raw backend data (all transactions, different date range, or including pending items). A $22K difference is too big to be a rounding issue — something is being excluded or double-counted on one side.
Where to look:
First, check if your balance sheet is filtering by date range and RIDGE is pulling everything. If your frontend has a date filter set, it might be excluding transactions that the backend query is including.
Second, check if bank transactions from Plaid are being counted differently than manual entries. If your cash balance on the balance sheet pulls from one table but RIDGE reads from another, that's your gap.
Third, check for pending vs. posted transactions. Plaid sometimes sends pending transactions that later get replaced by posted ones. If one system counts both and the other only counts posted, you get a mismatch.
