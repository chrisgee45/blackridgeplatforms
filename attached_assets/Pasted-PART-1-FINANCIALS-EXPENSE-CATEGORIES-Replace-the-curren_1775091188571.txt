Replace the current flat expense list with a structured category system. Create a expense_categories table with a group field and schedule_c_line field so every expense maps directly to a Schedule C tax line. Here are the categories, their group, and their Schedule C line:
Operating Expenses
	∙	Software & Subscriptions → Line 27a (Other)
	∙	Advertising & Marketing → Line 8
	∙	Office Supplies & Expenses → Line 18
	∙	Phone & Internet → Line 25
	∙	Utilities → Line 25
	∙	Rent / Workspace → Line 20b
People Costs
	∙	Contract Labor / Freelancers → Line 11
	∙	Employee Wages → Line 26
	∙	Commissions Paid → Line 10
Business Development
	∙	Travel & Lodging → Line 24a
	∙	Meals & Entertainment → Line 24b (flag: only 50% deductible — show deductible amount automatically)
	∙	Client Gifts → Line 27a (flag: IRS limit $25/person — show warning if any single entry exceeds $25)
Professional Services
	∙	Legal Fees → Line 17
	∙	Accounting / Bookkeeping → Line 17
	∙	Consulting → Line 11
Assets & Equipment
	∙	Equipment Purchases → Line 13
	∙	Depreciation → Line 13
	∙	Vehicle / Mileage → Line 9 (flag: show mileage rate input at $0.70/mile for 2026, calculate dollar value automatically)
Insurance
	∙	Business Insurance → Line 15
	∙	Health Insurance → Line 15 (flag: “May qualify for self-employed health insurance deduction — consult your CPA”)
	∙	Liability Insurance → Line 15
Financial Costs
	∙	Bank Fees → Line 27a
	∙	Merchant Processing Fees → Line 27a (Stripe fees, etc.)
	∙	Interest Paid → Line 16b
When recording an expense, the user selects a group first, then a category within that group. The Schedule C line maps automatically — never shown to the user, only used for the Tax Center and CPA export.
